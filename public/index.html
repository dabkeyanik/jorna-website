A short guide for whichever side of the aisle you're on.

Planning a wedding or a big event means juggling a dozen vendors — venue, caterer, DJ, dhol, mehndi artist, photographer — across calls, texts, and spreadsheets. Jorna brings all of it into one app: tell it about your event and it assembles complete vendor teams for you; vendors list their services and get discovered by people who are actively planning. Every payment is held safely until the event actually happens.

  1. Create your account

    Sign up with your email or continue with Google, then choose “I'm planning an event.” That's it — you're in.

  2. Build a bundle with the AI planner

    Answer a few quick questions — where the event is (city & state), how many guests, your date or date range, budget, and the vibe you're after. Jorna returns three ready-made vendor teams: Budget, Balanced, and Top Rated.

    Each bundle is a full line-up — venue, catering, DJ, and more — matched to the exact service you need, at one clear price. Don't love a pick? Swap any single vendor for another.

    Good to know. Bundles are “service-first,” so a DJ slot only ever shows DJs — never a dhol player by mistake.

  3. Or discover vendors yourself

    Prefer to browse? Search and filter by category, rating, and price, open a vendor's profile to see their work, prices, and reviews, then add their service to your bundle with your date, time, guest count, and — if it runs across days — a multi-day range.

  4. Book and pay — safely

    Send your requests. Vendors approve them, and for any service marked negotiable you can make an offer and settle on a price first. When you're ready, pay by card through Stripe.

    Here's the important part: your money doesn't go to the vendor yet. It's held in escrow by Jorna until the event happens. Priced per person or per day? Jorna does the math — rate × guests, or × days — so the total is always right.

  5. Chat with your whole team

    Every booking opens a group chat with your vendors. Messages arrive live, so last-minute details, timelines, and questions all live in one thread instead of a dozen texts.

  6. Confirm, and celebrate

    After the event, tap Confirm to release the payment — you're vouching the vendor delivered. Something went wrong? You can request a refund within 24 hours of paying, or Report a Problem to freeze that one booking for our team to review. Then leave a review to help the next host.

    Your safety net. Funds are never released before the event date, and a dispute always affects just that one booking — never the rest of your team.
For the vendor

Get discovered. Get booked. Get paid.

Reach clients who are actively planning, run every booking from one screen, and know your payout is guaranteed the moment you deliver.

Warm leads, not cold onesAppear in AI bundles and search for people ready to book.

One place to run itRequests, chats, calendar, and earnings together.

  1. Set up your vendor profile

    Sign up and choose “I'm a vendor.” Add a short bio and pick your category and specialty — DJ, dhol, catering, venue, mehndi artist, and more — so the right clients find you.

  2. List your services

    Add each service with photos, and price it the way you actually charge: per hour, per day, per event, or per person. Flip on “negotiable” for anything you're open to discussing. Running a venue? Drop a map pin so clients — and their other vendors — know exactly where to be.

    Why the unit matters. Jorna computes each booking's total from your rate and the client's details, so a per-person quote scales to their real guest count automatically.

  3. Turn on payments

    Connect your account through Stripe once. This is what lets Jorna pay you out — and clients can't complete a booking with you until it's set up, so it's worth doing early.

  4. Manage your requests

    When a client picks you — from an AI bundle or from search — the request lands in your Bookings tab. Approve it, decline it, or, on services you've marked negotiable, counter with a price until you both agree.

  5. Show up and get paid

    Once approved, the client pays — and that money sits safely in escrow with your name on it. On the day, open the booking at the venue and check in: your GPS-verified check-in is your confirmation that you delivered.

    When the client confirms too, your payout is released automatically — your amount, minus Jorna's platform fee.

    No venue to check into? Just tap Confirm instead — the client's confirmation vouches you delivered.

  6. Track earnings and stay booked

    Your Earnings view breaks down what's paid out, held in escrow, and still upcoming, with a full history. Keep your calendar current (and sync it with Google) so you never get double-booked, and watch your reviews build your reputation.
